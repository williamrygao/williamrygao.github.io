# Everything one might want to consider before majoring in math

<p class="font3 right">27 FEBRUARY 2026</p>

The last one is the most important.

* You will learn to beware of superstimuli or be swallowed by them forever.
* You will become fluent in French of the Grothendieck idiolect.
* 8 is a pretty big number.
* Your business friends will pressure you to be a quant, your cs friends will pressure you to work at Anthropic.
* You will load common English words beyond what is epistemically sustainable.
* Mathematical writing is the most dense kind of writing, and Serre's is the most transparent kind of writing.
* You will raise your bar for understanding beyond what is socially acceptable.
* Unfortunately, the world is not as funny as the one mathematicians have invented.
* You will learn about everyone else's research because they'll come running to you when any difficult math arises.
* Classrooms will smell (less than cs or stats, but more than average).
* You will develop a crippling tendency to generalize from one example.
